How It Started

What began as a learning project soon became something more. While experimenting and building just to learn HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I realized how often I — and many others — needed small, simple tools daily. That's when I decided to turn it into something real. EveryTool.tech was born out of curiosity but evolved out of a real need.

My Goals with EveryTool

My mission is simple: to build a fast, accurate, and free toolkit hub that anyone can use — whether you're a student, developer, creator, or just someone solving a quick task. I want this site to be a go-to platform for daily utility tools, a personal development journey log, and eventually, a monetizable product.

What I'm Learning

I'm actively learning web development — building this platform helped me explore real-world DOM manipulation, glassmorphism & animations, responsive UI/UX, and more. I mostly use ChatGPT as my main coding companion, and Gemini for deep research and advanced queries. AI has completely changed how I build, test, and learn.

Challenges I've Faced

It wasn't always smooth. Some of the trickiest things I faced were: styling consistent headers and footers, adding micro-animations that don't break responsiveness, structuring each tool page efficiently, and working around code size limits and manual linking due to a static setup. But every issue became a learning checkpoint.

My Vision for You

This site isn't made by a company. It's built by someone learning every day, improving little by little, and aiming to make useful things for the internet. I hope you find EveryTool simple to use, fast to load, and actually helpful. If even one tool helps you save time or solve something — that's a win for me.
